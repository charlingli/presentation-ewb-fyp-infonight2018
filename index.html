What is it?

EWB's research program brings together students, academics, and communities to conduct research with real-world impact.

How does it work?

  1. You choose a project and a Monash academic supervisor.
  2. You register with EWB Australia, who'll put you in touch with industry partners, field professionals, remote communities, and other students.
  3. Depending on your department, you'll need to submit a project proposal and a progress report along with a reverse brief to EWB Australia.
  4. As you do your FYP, both your supervisor and EWB Australia should provide mentoring and support.
  5. Depending on your department, you'll need to submit a final report, research paper, and final presentation along with a report summary to EWB Australia.
  6. Relevant materials will be forwarded by EWB Australia to industry partners, field professionals, remote communities, and other students.
  7. You could be asked to present your project at the Link Research Symposium or have it published in the Journal of Humanitarian Engineering!

Why do it?

  • Industry support
  • Holistic learning
  • Looks and sounds cool
  • Tangible impact
